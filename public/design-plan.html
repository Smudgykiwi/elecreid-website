STAGE SIX A · TV INSTALL
6A

TV install, properly done.

Clients buy their own TVs. We install and integrate them properly - wall-mounted, calibrated, cabled cleanly. The TV becomes part of the architecture, not a black rectangle stuck on a wall.

We supply the brackets, run the cabling through the wall to the joinery, mount and align every TV, calibrate the picture, and tie the sound and control into the rest of the home. Buying the TV separately keeps the warranty with the manufacturer where it belongs, and gives you flexibility on brand and model. We just need to know what you're putting on each wall.

Room TV? Under 65", flat Under 65", swing-out 65" or bigger, flat 65" or bigger, swing-out

We just need to know if any TV has a One Connect box / external control unit so we plan the cable run correctly.

STAGE SIX B · WHOLE-HOME AUDIO
6B

Music in every room.

Whole-home audio is the single most-used part of any smart home. Designed properly, it disappears into the architecture and follows you through the house.

Three audio levels, recommended room by room. Background fills a space without dominating it. Decent is proper everyday listening. Pumping is full-range, high-fidelity sound for when you want to feel it. Speakers can be visible or invisible. Invisible speakers are built into the ceiling or wall and plastered over, completely hidden. They cost more, but the result is stunning. We commission to a single app, with access to Sight & Sound Galleria as our preferred high-end installer for specialty two-channel listening systems.

Room Audio? Level 1 - Background Level 2 - Decent Level 3 - Pumping No audio Visible Invisible

Level 3 rooms include subwoofers as part of the spec. Full-range audio needs full-range bass.

STAGE SIX H · POOL
6H

Pool control, your way.

Two paths: keep it simple with the pool company's own app, or bring the pool into the rest of the home so it's all one experience.

Most pool builders prefer to install their own controller and hand you their app. That works, and it's the simpler path. Bringing the pool into the main home automation system gives you one app for everything: pool, lighting, blinds, climate, audio. The catch is real: it costs more, takes longer, and we need the pool builder to genuinely coordinate with us during construction. That means you need to back the brief and push them to play. Some builders welcome it. Others won't budge. We'll know early. The result, when it works, is worth it.
