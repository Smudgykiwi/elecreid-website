Choose your path.

All four platforms can deliver the same outcome. The difference is how they get there. Pick the path that suits your house, your habits, and the people who live in it.

IMG-07-APPLE-HOME
Apple Home app on iPhone, clean interface
Apple Home
BEST FOR
Families already in the Apple ecosystem.
STRENGTH
End to end encrypted. No cloud. No subscription. Native to every Apple device.
TRADE-OFF
Locked to Apple hardware family. Less depth than dealer platforms.
OUR TAKE
Our default for most residential smart homes. Effortless.
Select Apple Home
IMG-08-HOME-ASSISTANT
Home Assistant dashboard on screen, open source
Home Assistant
BEST FOR
Homeowners who want total control and zero vendor lock-in.
STRENGTH
Open source. Two thousand integrations. Runs locally on your hardware.
TRADE-OFF
Steeper learning curve. Not as polished out of the box.
OUR TAKE
For technically-minded clients who want to peek under the hood.
Select Home Assistant
IMG-09-CONTROL4
Control4 touchscreen interface, dealer installation
Control4
BEST FOR
Larger homes wanting professional-grade dealer integration.
STRENGTH
Tight integration. Dealer-only. Backed by Snap One. Built for scale.
TRADE-OFF
Subscription model. Less flexible for changes after handover.
OUR TAKE
For projects that need a dedicated platform with white-glove support.
Select Control4
IMG-10-BASALTE-HOME
Basalte switch on architectural wall, Belgian design
Basalte Home
BEST FOR
Architecturally-led projects where switches are part of the design.
STRENGTH
Hardware and software designed together. Belgian engineering.
TRADE-OFF
Locked to the Basalte hardware ecosystem. Premium pricing.
OUR TAKE
When the switch IS the architectural statement.
Select Basalte Home
STAGE THREE
03

Your home, on a screen.

Most homes have one main screen upstairs and one downstairs. These have full access to the house and serve as intercoms.

Smaller screens go in major rooms - bedrooms, living, office, kitchen - for quick local control of lights, blinds, climate, and music in that space.
